Sour Pickled Mustard Greens Soup with Beef (Canh dua chua bo)

Tempting to savour a delicious Beef stew-like dish? U have come to the right place.

Instructions

Here are the steps to prepare this dish:

  1. Prepare the Beef:
    • Choose short ribs with a balance of lean meat, fat, and tendons.
    • Wash and cut into 3x3 cm cubes.
    • Marinate with fish sauce (1 tsp), salt (½ tsp), seasoning powder (1 tsp), pepper (1 tsp), and minced garlic. Mix well and let sit for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Mustard Greens & Other Ingredients:
    • Squeeze out excess liquid from pickled mustard greens (rinse if too sour). Cut into bite-sized pieces.
    • Reserve some pickling juice to enhance the soup’s flavor.
    • Cut tomatoes into wedges. Chop green onions. Slice shallots and mince garlic.
  3. Cook the Beef:
    • Sauté garlic in oil until fragrant, then add marinated beef. Stir-fry until slightly browned.
    • Transfer to a pot, add boiling water, and pour in some pickling juice.
    • Simmer on low heat until the beef is tender, skimming off foam occasionally.
  4. Sauté the Mustard Greens:
    • In a separate pan, sauté shallots, then add mustard greens and ⅔ of the tomatoes.
    • Stir-fry until soft, adding a bit of sugar to balance the sourness.
  5. Combine & Simmer:
    • Add the sautéed greens to the beef pot. Simmer for 45-50 minutes until the beef is tender and flavors meld.
    • Add the remaining ⅓ of the tomatoes for color and texture. Simmer for a few more minutes.
  6. Enjoy!
    • Traditionally, this soup is best when cooked twice: first simmered, then cooled and reheated before serving.
    • Before serving, adjust seasoning, add green onions, sprinkle with pepper, and serve hot.
